STARHAUL Alpha Balance Targets
==============================

Current pass goals
------------------
- Raw mining should keep the player solvent, not rich.
- Refining should beat raw selling by a noticeable margin.
- Missions should be a major source of progression.
- Combat and derelict rewards should feel spiky and exciting, not constant.
- Early upgrades should be earned over several station runs, not one or two.
- Pilot leveling should feel slower and more deliberate.

Target pacing
-------------
- First ship upgrade:
  - target 12-18 minutes for a new player
- First useful cargo / fuel / mining decision point:
  - target 20-30 minutes
- First faction rank-up:
  - target 20-35 minutes depending on mission focus
- First meaningful specialization in training:
  - target 35-50 minutes
- First strong map-travel confidence:
  - target 45-75 minutes

Economy targets
---------------
- Raw resource selling:
  - low and steady income
- Refining:
  - should outperform raw selling enough to feel worth docking for
- Mission payouts:
  - should be clearly better than free mining loops
- Rare finds:
  - should feel uncommon and exciting
- Boarding / derelict cache rewards:
  - should feel like bonus spikes, not routine income

Current alpha tuning notes
--------------------------
- Raw resource values reduced.
- Refined values reduced slightly, but remain better than raw.
- Rare item values reduced slightly.
- Rare drop chances reduced across standard nodes and derelicts.
- Refinery fees increased.
- Bounty tag cash payout reduced.
- Cargo, fuel, engine, and mining upgrades cost more.
- Cargo, fuel, engine, and mining upgrade gains are smaller per tier.
- Refuel, repair, restock, and storage expansion cost more.
- Mining and selling XP gains are lower.
- Mission XP conversion is lower.
- XP needed per level is higher.

Questions to answer during playtest
-----------------------------------
- How many full cargo runs does it take to buy:
  - engine upgrade 1
  - cargo upgrade 1
  - mining upgrade 1
  - fuel upgrade 1
- Does refining feel worth the station trip?
- Do rare drops feel rare enough now?
- Do combat repairs/restocks eat too much mission profit?
- Does level gain still feel too fast after 20-30 minutes?
- Does the player still default to cargo upgrades first every time?

Future tuning buckets
---------------------
- Raw mining values
- Refinery profitability
- Mission credit payouts
- Mission XP payouts
- Upgrade cost ramps
- Fuel and support service costs
- Combat loot and bounty desk values
- Faction rep gain pace
